Danielle Stibbe

Research assistant

Research Interests

I am particularly interested in the psychophysiology of fear conditioning. Understanding such processes can shed light on the development and possible treatments of various phobias. At the moment, I am assisting Lotte Stemerding in a project studying the ideal conditions required for memories to destabilize.

Background

I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Psychology at the University of Leiden (cum laude) in 2017. I recently graduated a Psychology Research Master at the University of Amsterdam with merit, majoring in Brain and Cognition and minoring in Clinical Psychology. While conducting an internship required for my Master’s degree, I joined the Emotion Memory Lab. Then, I studied two psychophysiological measures of fear conditioning and their reaction to stimulus revaluation.
